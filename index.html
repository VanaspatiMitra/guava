About the Crop:

The Guava (Psidium guajava) L-49 (Lakshman) variety begins yielding fruit in 2 to 3 years, with optimal production reached by the fourth or fifth year. It thrives in well-drained loamy soil with a pH range of 5 to 7.5. Each acre can yield around 20 to 25 tons of fruit annually under proper care, including adequate irrigation, which requires approximately 800-1,200 mm of water annually, adjusted for local rainfall. With a production cost of around ₹50,000 to ₹70,000 per acre, farmers can expect profit margins of ₹1.5 to ₹2 lakhs per acre, depending on market conditions and quality

  • Soil Composition: Sandy, Loamy and Clayey Soil with Ph 5.0 to 7.5
  • Water need per Acre: 800-1200 mm per acre
  • Potential Dangers: Guava Moth, Fusarium oxysporum, Colletotrichum spp.
  • Fertilizers: Farmyard Manure, Muriate of Potash, Iron Chelate
  • Pesticides: Dimethoate, Lambda-Cyhalothrin, Bifenthrin
  • Profit Per Acre: ₹3,30,000 to ₹7,00,000 per acre annually
